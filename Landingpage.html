Questions

Everything you need to know before you start training.

The Putting Method is built to make putting feel simple, measurable, and trainable — not complicated. Start with a baseline, find the leak, then practice with a clear reason behind every rep.

Simple by design You do not need a tour-level stroke to improve. You need better feedback, better calibration, and a practice system that connects to the scorecard.
Who is The Putting Method built for?
It is built for golfers who want putting to feel more measurable and less random. That includes competitive players, serious amateurs, juniors, college golfers, coaches, and any player who knows they are losing shots on the greens but does not know exactly what to practice.
Do I need special hardware or a launch monitor?
No. The platform is designed around practical training you can use on a putting green, at home, or during tournament prep. The goal is to make start line, speed, tempo, green reading, and pressure easier to measure without needing expensive equipment.
What makes this different from normal putting drills?
Most drills tell you what to do. The Putting Method helps you understand why you are doing it. Your practice connects back to your miss patterns, make rates, speed control, strokes gained trends, and the specific putts that are costing you shots.
Can this help if my stroke is already technically solid?
Yes. A solid stroke does not automatically create great on-course putting. Elite putting also requires speed calibration, green-reading confidence, capture-speed control, pressure transfer, and the ability to adjust to changing conditions.
How does the baseline diagnostic work?
The diagnostic identifies the most likely leak in your putting: start line, speed control, green reading, tempo, or pressure. From there, it gives you a focused first practice block instead of leaving you to guess what needs work.
What does the dashboard actually track?
The dashboard is designed to track strokes gained trends, make rates by distance, miss patterns around the hole, speed-control tendencies, and practice progress. The goal is to show whether your training is actually transferring to lower scores.
Is this for indoor practice or on-course use?
Both. The Tempo Trainer and drill structure help guide practice sessions, while the green-reading card and performance tracking are built to support real rounds, warm-ups, tournament prep, and post-round review.
Why focus so much on putting?
Because putting is the part of golf every player can realistically master. Not every golfer can drive it like a tour player, but almost every golfer has the physical ability to roll the ball on line with good speed. The difference is having a system that makes improvement repeatable.
